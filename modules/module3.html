Case Study 3. Lord Howe Stick Insects

This case study is on the Lord Howe Stick Insect which has been widely publicised by the Melbourne Zoo where they have been bred successfully in captivity. These insects are critically endangered and were on the brink of extinction in 2001. This case study is expected to take 2-4 hours of class time.

Summary

VCE Key Knowledge Biology Study Design (2017-2021)

Unit 3: How do cells maintain life?, Area of Study 1: How do cellular processes work?, Outcome 1 (VCE Biology Study Design)

Key knowledge:

  • Threats to biodiversity
    • predictions of species population survival using probabilities including likelihood of extinction
    • non-human threats to biodiversity including creation and isolation of small populations through habitat modification
    • assessment of threat in defining conservation categories including extinct in the wild
  • Protection and restoration of biodiversity
    • strategies for maintaining and growing populations that also build species resilience to changes in the environment, including: captive breeding and reintroduction programs

Duration

2-4 hours

Student learning outcomes

Upon completion of this module students will:

  • identify non-human threats that can cause environmental impact
  • describe and evaluate methods for protecting environments and managing healthy populations
  • define the different conservation categories giving examples of each
  • understand the importance of captive breeding in the restoration and reintroduction of a near extinct species
